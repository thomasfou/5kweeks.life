The idea

A life is roughly four thousand weeks. So spend them like they're rare.

That number — about 4,000 weeks for an average lifetime — is shockingly small written down. It's also a quiet gift: small enough to hold in your head, and to take seriously.

5k Weeks takes a more hopeful posture. With what we now know about movement, rest, purpose, and metabolic health, aiming for 5,000 weeks — around 96 years — or beyond is a goal worth living toward. The point isn't to fear the count. It's to make the weeks you have unmistakably yours.

Why the week

Don't optimize your day. Compose your week.

A day is too small to judge a life by — and far too easy to ruin. The week is the unit 5k Weeks is built around: forgiving, repeatable, and exactly the right size to live with intention.

i. the day

Too small to measure a life

One rough morning shouldn't define you. Day-level tracking turns living into a streak you're terrified to break.

ii. the week

The right unit of intention

Set a few meaningful goals on Monday. Review them on Friday. A bad day fits inside a good week — and the week still counts.

iii. the life

Made of weeks lived well

String together intentional weeks and the rest of your life takes care of itself — no matter what else happens.

What you track

Five habits. One fuller life.

Not fifty metrics — five habits, each independently linked to a longer, healthier, more meaningful life. Pick one to start. Add the rest when you're ready.

Goals

Up to five meaningful goals each week

Weekly reflection on what matters builds resilience and purpose — and a strong sense of purpose is associated with 15–20% lower mortality risk.

Potential gain: 4–7 years

Meditation

A few quiet minutes, most days

Brief daily sessions lower cortisol and inflammation and are linked to slower cellular aging through better-preserved telomeres.

Mortality reduction: 5–10%

Gratitude

Note what you're thankful for

Naming what you're grateful for activates the brain's reward centers — linked to better sleep, lower blood pressure, and less depression.

Potential gain: 5–7 years

Exercise

Move your body, your way

The single best-documented longevity habit there is: roughly 150 minutes of moderate movement a week cuts all-cause mortality by 20–35%.

Potential gain: up to 7 years

Fasting

Gentle time-restricted eating

Intermittent fasting triggers autophagy — your cells' built-in cleanup — and improves metabolic flexibility and insulin sensitivity.

Potential gain: 2–5 years

They compound.

Each habit helps alone — but the magic is the combination. Exercise makes fasting easier. Meditation sharpens your goals. Gratitude fuels motivation. Together they become a virtuous cycle, the same one Blue Zones research keeps finding behind the world's longest, happiest lives.

Good questions

Everything you might be wondering.

5k Weeks is a habit-tracking and life-calendar app for living intentionally. It shows your entire life as a grid of weeks and helps you keep five habits that matter — goals, meditation, gratitude, exercise, and fasting — so you finish each week feeling fulfilled. It's free to download for iPhone and Android.

The average human life lasts roughly 4,000 weeks — a number made famous by Oliver Burkeman's book Four Thousand Weeks. 5k Weeks takes a more hopeful view: with better habits and modern longevity science, aiming for 5,000 weeks (about 96 years) or beyond is a goal worth living toward. The name is both a reminder that time is finite and an invitation to make more of it.

Five habits: weekly goals, meditation, gratitude, exercise, and intermittent fasting. We chose these five because each is independently linked to a longer, healthier, more meaningful life — and because together they compound into a virtuous cycle. That's deliberately a short list. The goal is to steer your life, not audit it.

5k Weeks doesn't try to measure everything — apps like Apple Health already do that beautifully. It's the one place you go to set intentions and steer, not to log every metric. It zooms out from the day to the week to your whole life, so you spend less time staring at data and more time living deliberately.

A single day is too small to judge a life by, and too easy to ruin. A week is forgiving enough to absorb a bad day yet short enough to act on. 5k Weeks treats the week as the unit of an intentional life: live each week well, and the rest of your life tends to take care of itself — no matter what happens.
